Gangnam Clinic Guide for Foreigners: Korean Dermatology & Aesthetic Procedures in 2026

Published February 2026 · By Glowmi Team · 10 min read

South Korea is the world capital of aesthetic dermatology, and Gangnam district in Seoul is its epicenter. Every year, hundreds of thousands of international visitors travel to Gangnam specifically for cosmetic and dermatological procedures that cost a fraction of Western prices while delivering world-class results. This guide covers everything a foreigner needs to know before booking a clinic visit.

Why Gangnam? The Global Aesthetic Capital

Gangnam-gu alone has over 500 registered dermatology and plastic surgery clinics packed into a few square kilometers. The concentration of specialists here is unmatched anywhere in the world. Korean dermatologists perform more aesthetic procedures per capita than practitioners in any other country, meaning their experience levels are extraordinarily high.

The competitive density of clinics in Gangnam drives prices down and quality up. Clinics invest in the latest technology from companies like Classys (Ultraformer), Jeisys (INTRAcel), and Lutronic (Hollywood Spectra) — many of these devices were invented in Korea. You will have access to treatments and equipment that may not yet be available in your home country.

Medical tourism infrastructure is also well developed. Many clinics have multilingual coordinators, and the Korean government actively supports the medical tourism industry through the Korea Medical Tourism Association (KMED).

Most Popular Procedures for Foreigners

Below are the four most commonly requested procedures by international visitors, with typical 2026 price ranges at reputable Gangnam clinics.

Botulinum Toxin (Botox / Nabota / Xeomin)

Korea uses locally manufactured botulinum toxins like Nabota (Daewoong) and Meditoxin (Medytox) alongside international brands. These are KFDA-approved and widely used domestically. Treatments target forehead lines, crow's feet, jaw reduction (masseter Botox), and the popular "lip flip." Sessions take 10-15 minutes with zero downtime.

Korean clinics are especially skilled at masseter Botox for facial slimming — a procedure that is far more commonly requested in Korea than in the West. Results appear within 2-4 weeks and last 4-6 months.

50,000 - 200,000 KRW~$35 - $140 USDNo Downtime10-15 min

Dermal Fillers (Hyaluronic Acid)

Filler treatments in Korea use premium hyaluronic acid brands including Juvederm, Restylane, and Korean-made options like Elravie and Chaeum. Popular areas include the nose bridge (non-surgical rhinoplasty), under-eye hollows, nasolabial folds, chin augmentation, and lip enhancement.

Korean injectors are known for their subtle, natural-looking results — the "no filler" filler look. Clinics often use blunt-tip cannulas to minimize bruising. Results are immediate and last 6-18 months depending on the area and product used.

200,000 - 600,000 KRW~$140 - $420 USDMinimal Downtime20-30 min

Laser Toning (Pico / Nd:YAG)

Laser toning is the cornerstone of Korean skin maintenance. Using devices like the PicoSure, PicoWay, or Hollywood Spectra, dermatologists deliver low-fluence laser energy across the entire face to address pigmentation, melasma, enlarged pores, and overall skin texture. Most Koreans get laser toning monthly as part of their regular skincare regimen.

A single session takes 15-20 minutes. You may experience mild redness for a few hours. For best results, clinics recommend a series of 5-10 sessions, but even a single treatment will leave your skin noticeably brighter and smoother. This is an excellent "lunch break" procedure for tourists.

80,000 - 150,000 KRW~$55 - $105 USDMild Redness15-20 min

Skin Boosters (Rejuran / Salmon DNA / Juvelook)

Skin boosters are micro-injections of hydrating and regenerative compounds directly into the dermis. Rejuran Healer (polynucleotide from salmon DNA) is Korea's signature treatment — virtually unknown in many Western countries but a staple in Korean dermatology. Juvelook, a newer PDLLA-based booster, stimulates collagen over time.

These treatments improve skin elasticity, hydration, fine lines, and overall "glass skin" luminosity from within. Expect tiny needle marks that fade in 1-2 days. A course of 3 sessions spaced 2-4 weeks apart delivers the most dramatic results.

What to Expect at a Gangnam Clinic

The typical clinic visit follows a consistent pattern. You will arrive and fill out a consultation form (many clinics provide English, Chinese, and Japanese versions). A coordinator will guide you to a consultation room where you meet the dermatologist. After examining your skin — often using a VISIA or skin analysis device — the doctor recommends a treatment plan.

If you agree to proceed, treatment usually happens the same day. Payment is made upfront, and most clinics accept international credit cards. After the procedure, the staff will explain aftercare instructions and provide take-home products if needed. The entire visit typically takes 1-2 hours including wait time.

Navigating the Language Barrier

Language is the biggest concern for most foreign visitors, but the situation has improved dramatically. Here are practical strategies:

  • International clinics: Many Gangnam clinics have full-time English, Chinese, or Japanese-speaking coordinators. Search for clinics that specifically advertise international patient services.
  • Papago / Google Translate: Naver's Papago app handles Korean-English medical terminology surprisingly well. Download it before your trip and use the conversation mode during consultations.
  • Medical tourism agencies: Companies like Seoul Guide Medical and My Seoul Secret provide free consultation booking, clinic vetting, and in-person interpretation services. They are paid by the clinics, not by you.
  • Prepare a visual reference: Bring reference photos of the results you want. Visual communication transcends language barriers and helps the doctor understand your aesthetic goals precisely.
  • Written treatment plan: Always request a written summary of the planned procedures, products to be used, and total cost before consenting to treatment.

Consultation Tips for Best Results

A good consultation is the foundation of a successful treatment. Korean doctors tend to be conservative and may actually talk you out of procedures they do not think you need. Here is how to make the most of your consultation:

  • Be specific about your concerns: Instead of saying "make me look better," point to the exact areas that bother you. Korean doctors appreciate precision.
  • Ask about the doctor's experience: How many times have they performed this specific procedure? Reputable doctors will answer openly.
  • Discuss alternatives: Korean clinics often offer combination treatments. Laser toning plus a skin booster, for example, may deliver better results than either alone.
  • Understand the timeline: Some results are immediate (fillers); others take weeks (Botox, skin boosters). Plan your visit accordingly.
  • Get a second opinion: Gangnam clinics are so close together that you can easily visit 2-3 for consultations in a single afternoon. Consultations are usually free or very low cost.

Safety Checks: How to Spot a Reputable Clinic

While Gangnam has world-class clinics, the sheer number means quality varies. Protect yourself with these verification steps:

  • KFDA registration: Verify the clinic is registered with the Korean Ministry of Health and Welfare. The official medical institution search is available at the HIRA website (hira.or.kr).
  • Doctor credentials: Confirm the treating doctor is a board-certified dermatologist (피부과 전문의) or plastic surgeon (성형외과 전문의), not a general practitioner.
  • "Ghost doctor" risk: In rare cases, a different doctor than the one you consulted may perform the procedure. Ask directly: "Will YOU personally be performing my treatment?"
  • Product authenticity: Ask to see the sealed packaging of injectables before they are opened. Reputable clinics use genuine, KFDA-approved products and will show you the packaging.
  • Online reviews: Check Korean platforms like Gangnam Unni (강남언니), Babitalk, and Yeoshin for Korean patient reviews. Google reviews alone are not sufficient — Korean patients leave the most detailed feedback on local apps.
  • Avoid suspiciously low prices: If a clinic quotes prices significantly below the ranges listed above, question what products they are using. Extremely cheap fillers may be unbranded or diluted.

Recovery and Aftercare

Most non-surgical procedures have minimal downtime, but smart planning makes recovery smoother:

  • Botox: No downtime. Avoid lying flat for 4 hours and skip intense exercise for 24 hours. Do not massage the treated area.
  • Fillers: Mild swelling and possible bruising for 2-5 days. Avoid alcohol for 24 hours. Sleep elevated the first night.
  • Laser toning: Mild redness for a few hours. Apply sunscreen religiously for the next 2 weeks. Avoid direct sun exposure.
  • Skin boosters: Tiny needle marks and mild swelling for 1-2 days. No makeup for 12 hours. Hydrate generously.

Most clinics will provide aftercare products and detailed written instructions. If you are flying home within a few days, inform your doctor — they may adjust the treatment plan to ensure you are comfortable for travel.

Insurance and Payment

Aesthetic procedures are considered elective and are not covered by Korean national health insurance or most international travel insurance. All costs are out-of-pocket. However, compared to equivalent procedures in the US, Europe, or Australia, you will typically save 50-70% even after factoring in travel expenses.

Most clinics accept Visa, Mastercard, and UnionPay. Some offer tax refund services for foreign tourists (you can reclaim the 10% VAT at the airport). Always confirm the total price in writing before the procedure, including any follow-up fees.

Carry your passport — clinics are legally required to verify foreign patient identity, and you will need it for the tax refund paperwork.

How to Find the Right Clinic

Finding a reputable Gangnam clinic as a foreigner can be overwhelming. Here is a step-by-step approach:

  • Step 1: Identify your target procedure and research which clinics specialize in it. General clinics that do everything are rarely the best at anything.
  • Step 2: Cross-reference reviews on Gangnam Unni, Google, and Reddit (r/PlasticSurgery, r/KoreanBeauty). Look for consistent positive feedback over time, not just a handful of glowing reviews.
  • Step 3: Contact 2-3 clinics via KakaoTalk or email with your concerns and photos. Compare their recommendations and pricing.
  • Step 4: Schedule consultations for the same day so you can compare in person. Gangnam clinics are walkable from each other.
  • Step 5: Trust your instinct. A good clinic will not pressure you, will explain risks honestly, and will be transparent about pricing.

Final Tips for Your Gangnam Trip

  • Best time to visit: Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) offer mild weather that is ideal for post-procedure recovery. Summer humidity and winter dryness can complicate healing.
  • Stay in Gangnam: Book accommodation within walking distance of your clinic. Subway stations Gangnam, Sinnonhyeon, and Apgujeong are the key clinic hubs.
  • Schedule treatments early in your trip: This gives you time for follow-up visits if needed and lets initial swelling or redness subside before you fly home.
  • Download KakaoTalk: This is Korea's primary messaging app and the main way clinics communicate. Install it and add your clinic's contact before arrival.
  • Bring your skincare routine list: Your doctor will want to know what active ingredients you currently use, especially retinoids and acids, as these can affect treatment plans.

Gangnam's aesthetic clinics offer an incredible combination of expertise, technology, and affordability. With proper research and preparation, a clinic visit can be the highlight of your Korean beauty journey. The key is doing your homework, asking the right questions, and choosing a specialist who understands your goals.
